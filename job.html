Waiter/Waitress Guide

📌 Verbs (Actions You’ll Do All the Time)

  • Tomar nota – To take an order ("Voy a tomar nota" = "I’ll take your order").
  • Apuntar – Same as above, but more casual ("¿Te apunto algo?" = "Can I get you something?").
  • Traer – To bring ("Te traigo más pan" = "I’ll bring you more bread").
  • Cobrar – To charge/pay the bill ("¿Quieres que te cobremos ya?" = "Do you want to pay now?").
  • Dejar propina – To leave a tip (super important!).
  • Repartir – To serve/distribute ("Voy a repartir las bebidas" = "I’ll serve the drinks").
  • Ir/venir pitando – To go/come quickly ("Voy pitando con tu café" = "I’ll bring your coffee ASAP").

🍽️ Phrases for Taking Orders

  • "¿Qué os ponemos?" – "What can I get you?" (super common in Spain).
  • "¿Lo queréis para compartir?" – "Do you want to share it?"
  • "¿Algo más?" – "Anything else?" (short and sweet).
  • "¿Cómo lo queréis? ¿Poco hecho, al punto o bien hecho?" – "How do you want it? Rare, medium, or well done?" (for meat).
  • "¿Con hielo y limón?" – "With ice and lemon?" (for drinks like white wine or gin-tonic).

💰 Money & Bills

  • "¿Juntamos o separamos?" – "Together or separate?" (when splitting the bill).
  • "Son 25 pavos" – "It’s 25 bucks" (pavos = slang for euros in Spain).
  • "¿Llevas la cuenta?" – "Do you have the bill?" (what customers might ask).
  • "¿Te pongo la cuenta?" – "Shall I bring the bill?"

😊 Customer Service Phrases

  • "En un momentito" – "Just a sec" (when you’re busy but want to reassure them).
  • "Cuidado, que quema" – "Careful, it’s hot!" (for soups, coffee, etc.).
  • "¿Todo bien?" / "¿Va todo bien?" – "Everything okay?" (checking in).
  • "¡Que aproveche!" – "Enjoy your meal!" (say this after serving food).

🙄 Annoying Situations (and How to Handle Them)

  • "Me han traído esto frío" – "They brought me this cold" → "Ahora te lo cambio, perdona" ("I’ll change it now, sorry").
  • "¿Dónde está mi pedido?" – "Where’s my order?" → "Ahora miro en cocina" ("I’ll check the kitchen now").
  • "No me gusta esto" – "I don’t like this" → "¿Quieres que te traiga otra cosa?" ("Do you want me to bring you something else?").

🍻 Bar/Café Slang (Extra Fun!)

  • Un cortado – Espresso with a dash of milk.
  • Un café solo – Black coffee (strong!).
  • Una caña – A small beer (very typical in Spain).
  • Un chupito – A shot (of liquor).
  • "Poner una ronda" – To buy a round of drinks ("¿Ponemos otra ronda?" = "Shall we get another round?").
